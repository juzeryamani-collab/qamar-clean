Technical Spotlight

What MRI Suite Construction Actually Requires

MRI construction is one of the most technically demanding projects in commercial construction. Most GCs have never done one. We have built multiple MRI suites across Greater Houston.

MRI Suite Technical Requirements

RF Shielding
Copper mesh Faraday cage — 6-sided including floor/ceiling
Structural
Slab reinforcement for 4–20 ton magnet; seismic isolation
Cryogen
Quench pipe: dedicated route to exterior, no obstructions
Electrical
Dedicated 480V service; no ferrous conduit in exclusion zone
HVAC
±1°F temp stability; humidity 40–60%; no metallic ductwork in zone
Doors
RF-shielded door with wave guide; minimum 48" clear opening
Plumbing
No metallic pipe within Gauss line; non-ferrous alternatives
Control Room
Copper RF-shielded window; patient intercom system

Why This Is Critical

A single design error in MRI suite construction can cost $200,000–$500,000 to fix post-installation. Common mistakes by inexperienced contractors include:

❌ Ferrous conduit inside the Gauss exclusion zone — requires complete demo and rerouting
❌ Insufficient structural reinforcement — slab cracking under magnet weight
❌ Quench pipe routed through occupied space — safety hazard and code violation
❌ RF shield breach from unshielded penetrations — RF leakage fails acceptance testing
Our Process
We engage the MRI manufacturer's site planning team before design, coordinate all penetrations through the RF shield, and perform RF acceptance testing before delivery. Zero re-work on our MRI projects.

Our Process

How We Build Complex Medical Facilities

01

Regulatory Pre-Planning

Before a single drawing is produced, we identify every regulatory body involved — TDSHS, CMS, COH, NFPA — and establish concurrent submission timelines to compress your schedule.

02

Equipment Vendor Coordination

We obtain site planning documents from every equipment vendor (MRI, CT, imaging systems) and incorporate all infrastructure requirements into the design before permits are submitted.

03

Permit Concurrent Submission

We submit to all agencies simultaneously — City of Houston building permit, TDSHS radiation shielding, and CMS documentation — to run timelines in parallel, not series.

04

Critical Infrastructure First

Structural reinforcement, shielding, medical gas rough-ins, and HVAC infrastructure are sequenced first so downstream work is never held waiting for long-lead items.

05

Systems Verification

All medical gas systems are tested to NFPA 99. RF shielding is verified by acceptance test. Radiation shielding is surveyed by licensed physicist before occupancy.

06

Certificate of Occupancy & Accreditation Support

We prepare a complete closeout package — as-built drawings, O&M manuals, test reports, TDSHS certificates — ready for your AAAHC or Joint Commission accreditation survey.
