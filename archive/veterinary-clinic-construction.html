WHY VETERINARY CONSTRUCTION IS DIFFERENT

Vet Clinics Demand Infrastructure That Most GCs Don't Know

Veterinary facilities combine elements of medical, commercial, and industrial construction in ways that require real specialist experience. A general contractor who hasn't built vet clinics before will underestimate the complexity — and you'll pay for it in change orders and delays.

🔧
Heavy-Duty Plumbing Systems
Wet treatment rooms, surgical tables, hydrobath stations, kennel drains — all require commercial floor drains, grease interceptors, and acid-resistant waste lines.
💨
Isolation & Surgical HVAC
Surgical suites need 20+ air changes/hour with directional positive pressure. Isolation wards need negative pressure to prevent pathogen spread.
🔇
Sound Attenuation
Kennel barking noise must be contained. We use mass-loaded vinyl, acoustic insulation, and double-stud walls to protect exam rooms and reception from boarding noise.
🛡️
Infection Control Surfaces
Seamless epoxy flooring, wall panels with no grout lines, and stainless steel casework that can be disinfected without damage — a standard vet clinic requires across treatment, surgery, and kennel zones.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Veterinary Clinic Construction Questions Answered

How much does it cost to build a veterinary clinic in Houston?
Veterinary clinic construction in Houston typically ranges from $220,000–$380,000 for a smaller 3-exam-room general practice (1,400–1,800 sq ft) up to $580,000–$1.0M+ for a full animal hospital with surgical suite, ICU, and in-house diagnostics. Specialty hospitals and emergency facilities can reach $1.5–$1.8M. Contact us for a free site-specific estimate.
What special construction requirements does a vet clinic need?
Veterinary clinics require specialized construction including: seamless sealed flooring for infection control, commercial floor drains and grease interceptors in treatment areas, enhanced HVAC with high air exchange rates for surgical suites, negative pressure isolation wards, acoustic wall systems for kennel noise control, medical gas rough-ins for anesthesia, and lead-lined radiology rooms if X-ray equipment is included.
How long does vet clinic construction take in Houston?
A standard veterinary clinic build-out in Houston takes 12–20 weeks from permit approval to certificate of occupancy. Permitting in Harris County and surrounding counties takes 4–8 weeks. Larger animal hospitals or facilities with surgical suites may take 20–30 weeks. We plan your build backward from your target opening date.
Do you handle permits for vet clinics in Houston?
Yes. Qamar Construction manages all permitting on your behalf including City of Houston permits (or applicable county permits), mechanical, electrical, plumbing sub-permits, radiology room permits from TDSHS if X-ray is included, and the final certificate of occupancy for your use type.
Do you coordinate with veterinary equipment vendors?
Absolutely. We coordinate directly with your vet equipment vendors to ensure all rough-ins — medical gas, electrical, plumbing, and structural supports for surgical tables, imaging equipment, and lab systems — are installed exactly to manufacturer specs before walls close. This eliminates costly change orders later.
