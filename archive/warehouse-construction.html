WAREHOUSE TYPES WE BUILD

Industrial & Warehouse Construction Across Greater Houston

Houston is one of the most active industrial real estate markets in the country. We've been building warehouse and industrial spaces here for 25 years — from small flex units to large distribution facilities.

📦

Distribution Center

High-bay distribution facilities with multiple dock doors, drive-in ramps, heavy floor loads, and dense racking infrastructure.

  • 28–40 ft clear height construction
  • T5/T8 high-bay lighting systems
  • Multiple dock door layouts
  • ESFR fire suppression systems
  • Heavy-duty concrete slab design
🏢

Office / Warehouse Combo

Front-office showroom or corporate space combined with warehouse storage or light manufacturing behind — a Houston industrial classic.

  • Integrated office build-out
  • Grade-level & dock-high doors
  • Separate HVAC zoning
  • Clean finish office transition
  • Customer / staff parking design
🔧

Light Manufacturing / Flex

Industrial flex space for light manufacturing, assembly, fabrication, or service operations with 18–24 ft clear heights.

  • Heavy 3-phase electrical service
  • Crane runway beam rough-in
  • Industrial exhaust ventilation
  • Compressed air stub-outs
  • Floor drains & epoxy flooring
❄️

Cold Storage & Refrigerated

Temperature-controlled warehouse and cold storage facilities for food, pharmaceutical, or industrial applications.

  • Insulated panel wall systems
  • Refrigeration equipment rough-in
  • Anti-condensation vapor barriers
  • Specialized hardened flooring
  • Temperature-zoned HVAC design
🏗️

Pre-Engineered Metal Building

Cost-effective PEMB (pre-engineered metal building) construction for warehouses, equipment storage, and industrial use.

  • Rapid procurement & erection
  • Insulated metal panel systems
  • Skylights & ventilation design
  • Concrete foundation & slab
  • Interior fit-out if required
🧱

Tilt-Wall Construction

Tilt-wall (tilt-up) concrete panel construction for medium to large warehouse and industrial facilities throughout Greater Houston.

  • Tilt-wall panel engineering coordination
  • On-slab panel casting
  • Panel crane erection
  • Full interior fit-out
  • Dock, drive-in & loading design

FREQUENTLY ASKED

Warehouse Construction Questions

Warehouse shell construction in Houston ranges from $50–$85/sq ft for pre-engineered metal buildings to $65–$110/sq ft for tilt-wall construction. Distribution centers with dock equipment, sprinklers, and HVAC run $85–$130/sq ft. Office/warehouse combos add office build-out cost to the warehouse shell. Cold storage runs $120–$200/sq ft due to insulation and refrigeration systems. Site conditions, foundation requirements, and clear height all significantly impact final cost.

Tilt-wall construction casts concrete panels on-site and tilts them into place — ideal for buildings over 50,000 sq ft where the economy of scale works. Pre-engineered metal buildings are factory-fabricated steel structures — faster to erect, more cost-effective for 5,000–50,000 sq ft buildings, and highly customizable. We help you choose the right system for your size, budget, and operational requirements.

A common industry benchmark is one dock door per 5,000–10,000 sq ft depending on throughput. For a 30,000 sq ft distribution center with moderate activity, 3–6 dock doors is typical. High-velocity e-commerce distribution requires more. We help you right-size your dock count based on your projected truck volume, carrier requirements, and lease terms so you're not retrofitting expensive dock openings later.

Yes. Houston's subtropical climate — high humidity, frequent heavy rain, and occasional severe weather — requires specific design attention: proper site drainage and detention, HVAC sizing for high cooling loads, vapor barriers in concrete slabs, hurricane-rated door and panel systems, and roof systems designed for Houston's wind and rain exposure. We've built in Houston for 25 years and design for these conditions from the start.

Yes — permit management is included in our scope. For Houston-area warehouse projects we manage CoH building permits (or Harris/Fort Bend/Montgomery county permits for unincorporated areas), fire department plan review and fire suppression permits, electrical permits, and any HCFCD or TxDOT approvals required. We've submitted hundreds of commercial permit packages in this market and know exactly what each agency needs.
