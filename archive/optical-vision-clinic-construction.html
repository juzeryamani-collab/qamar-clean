Room-by-Room Expertise

Every Zone of Your Vision Clinic — Done Right

Optical clinics have lighting, layout, and technical requirements that standard commercial contractors don't understand. We've built them across Houston.

👁️

Exam Lanes

Properly proportioned exam lanes with precision lighting control for dilation exams and full equipment provisions.

  • Dimmable lighting (0–100% range)
  • 14-ft depth for projector chart systems
  • Chair and instrument power provisions
  • Data/network at every lane
  • Plumbing rough-in where required
⏳

Pre-Test & Dilation Waiting

Separate pre-test rooms and dilation lounges keep patient flow moving efficiently without bottlenecks in primary waiting.

  • Pre-test screening room with equipment power
  • Dilation lounge with low-light design
  • Separated from primary waiting area
  • ADA-compliant circulation throughout
🏪

Optical Dispensary

Retail dispensary seamlessly integrated with clinical space — lighting designed for both clinical assessment and retail frame display.

  • Frame wall systems and display lighting
  • Contact lens fitting station
  • Lab/finishing room adjacency
  • POS and data provisions
🔬

Diagnostic Imaging Room

Retinal imaging, OCT, fundus photography, and x-ray rooms with appropriate shielding and equipment provisions.

  • TDSHS radiation permit (x-ray/imaging)
  • Lead shielding where required
  • Imaging equipment power provisions
  • Darkened room capability
💉

Procedure Room (Ophthalmology)

Injection suites, laser treatment rooms, and minor procedure areas for ophthalmology practices.

  • Positive pressure ventilation
  • Surgical lighting power provisions
  • Medical gas rough-ins (O2)
  • HEPA filtration where indicated
🔧

Optical Finishing Lab

In-house lens cutting and edging lab with dedicated ventilation, utilities, and optimized workflow design.

  • Dedicated power for edging equipment
  • Lens grinding dust ventilation
  • Wet station with plumbing
  • Uncut lens inventory storage

FAQs

Optical Clinic Construction FAQs

Optometry office construction in Houston ranges from $175,000–$280,000 for a solo practice (2 exam lanes, 1,200–1,800 sq ft) to $320,000–$520,000 for a multi-doctor group practice (4–5 lanes, 2,500–3,800 sq ft) with full retail dispensary. Ophthalmology practices with procedure rooms run $550,000–$1.2M+.

Standard exam lanes are 8–10 ft wide × 12–14 ft deep (14 ft minimum for projector chart systems). Each requires dimmable lighting (0–100% range for dilation exams), dedicated power circuits for chair and instrument towers, data connections, and in some cases plumbing rough-in. Lighting control is critical — inadequate dimming compromises the dilation exam workflow.

Yes. We specialize in integrated clinic and dispensary spaces that optimize the patient journey from exam chair to frame selection. We design separate lighting zones — clinical white light for exams vs. accent lighting for retail display — and integrate dispensary, contact lens station, and in-house lab as part of the overall build.

A standard optometry office build-out takes 12–18 weeks including permitting. Larger clinics with procedure rooms may take 18–26 weeks. We submit permits and begin procurement simultaneously to compress your opening timeline. We're experienced with the Houston permitting process and know how to avoid common delays.

Yes. Any x-ray or radiation-producing equipment requires a TDSHS radiation shielding permit in Texas. We manage the complete process — shielding design, permit application, permit tracking, post-construction survey by a licensed medical physicist, and final equipment registration with TDSHS.
