How We Work

The 6-Phase Turn-Key Process
— In Complete Detail

Every Qamar project follows this exact process — transparent, predictable, and accountable at every step.

Days 1–5

Phase 1: Free Consultation

We meet — on-site, at your office, or virtually — to understand your project completely. We ask questions most contractors don't think to ask: How will your staff flow through this space? What does your patient or customer experience need to feel like? What's your actual budget ceiling — not the number you tell contractors, the real number? What's your opening date commitment?

We give you our honest assessment: what's realistic, what we'd recommend changing, and where we see risk. No pressure. No obligation.

  • Site walkthrough (in person)
  • Vision & functional requirements
  • Preliminary budget feasibility
  • Preliminary timeline assessment
  • Immediate written summary to you
Weeks 1–3

Phase 2: Design & Planning

Our design team creates architectural drawings, floor plans, 3D renderings, and full construction documents — all reviewed simultaneously by our construction team. This is where turn-key saves you money: when the person designing the space also knows what it costs to build, costly surprises get caught at the drawing stage when changing a line is free, not during construction when changes cost thousands.

You review and approve all design elements. We incorporate your feedback. We submit permit-ready documents at the end of this phase.

  • Schematic floor plans
  • 3D rendering for visualization
  • Finish & material selections
  • Equipment coordination rough-ins
  • Permit-ready construction documents
Weeks 2–4

Phase 3: Custom Quote & Contract

We deliver your quote in 24–48 hours from the time drawings are finalized. Not a rough estimate — a fully itemized, line-by-line breakdown of every cost: materials, labor, permits, MEP subs, and contingency. You know exactly what you're getting and exactly what it costs.

Our contract is a single agreement that covers design, permitting, and all construction. One document. No scope gaps between documents. No coordination risk between separate contracts.

  • Line-item cost transparency
  • Fixed-price contract (no surprises)
  • Clearly defined project scope
  • Payment schedule outline
  • Project timeline with milestones
Weeks 4–10

Phase 4: Permits & Pre-Construction

This is where most projects get delayed — not because of construction, but because of permitting. We have managed the permitting process for 100+ projects across Houston, Harris County, Fort Bend County, Montgomery County, Galveston County, and surrounding jurisdictions. We know the reviewers, the common comment cycles, and how to write documents that get approved the first time.

While permits are processing, we finalize sub-contractor bids, lock in material procurement, and prepare the site — so we're ready to break ground the day permits are issued.

  • City/county building permit
  • MEP sub-permits
  • TDLR (healthcare qualifying projects)
  • DSHS radiology (dental/imaging)
  • Fire marshal pre-submission review
Weeks 10–26

Phase 5: Construction

Our crews build your project with the precision of a team that designed it. There are no "surprises" in the drawings because we drew the drawings. There are no arguments about scope because we wrote the scope. Every trade — demo, framing, plumbing, electrical, HVAC, drywall, finishes, millwork — is managed by our team on your timeline.

You receive weekly progress updates with photos. You can call Zain directly anytime. We proactively communicate any issue — never wait for you to ask.

  • Demo & site preparation
  • Structural framing
  • MEP rough-in (plumbing, electrical, HVAC)
  • Drywall, taping & finish work
  • Flooring, millwork & cabinetry
  • Final finishes & painting
Final Week

Phase 6: Opening Day

Final inspections. Certificate of occupancy. Punch list walkthrough with you — item by item. Every deficiency corrected before we hand you the keys. Not after, not "we'll come back for that" — before. We stay until it's right.

On opening day, your space is finished, clean, inspected, permitted, and ready for business. We don't celebrate until you do.

  • Final building inspection
  • Certificate of occupancy obtained
  • Punch list walkthrough & clearance
  • Site cleanup & final delivery
  • Keys handed over — on your opening date
Turn-Key in Practice

The PCP For Life Renovation:
Turn-Key at Its Most Complex

The Challenge: PCP For Life is a primary care clinic operating 7 days a week, serving hundreds of patients weekly. They needed a complete interior renovation — but could not close for construction. Every patient day lost was revenue lost and trust eroded with their community.

The Qamar Turn-Key Solution: We designed the renovation in phases specifically mapped to the clinic's operational zones. Phase 1 tackled the back-of-house and a set of exam rooms. While those rooms were under construction, the clinic operated fully from remaining rooms. Phase 2 began as Phase 1 was completed. We coordinated infection control barriers, scheduled all disruptive work outside patient hours, and managed the entire sequencing — with daily communication to the clinical management team.

The Result: Four phases. Zero patient days lost. Full clinic transformation. On time. On budget. Dr. Karimjee's words: "Best construction team in town."

"The clinic was operational 7 days a week and Zain's team managed to renovate it in 4 phases without interrupting patient care. Best construction team in town." — Dr. Karimjee, PCP For Life

Turn-Key Design-Build Questions Answered

Turn-key design-build means one company handles the entire project from architectural design through final construction — delivering a fully finished, ready-to-occupy space. You sign one contract with one company, have one point of contact, and receive full accountability for every phase from concept to keys in hand.
In traditional construction, the owner hires an architect and a GC separately — two contracts, two accountable parties. When something goes wrong, you mediate between them. Design-build puts everything under one contract. The design team and build team are the same people, so design decisions are informed by real construction knowledge from day one.
Often less, not more. When design and construction are integrated, designs are built to be constructable and cost-efficient. Change orders from design errors — the single biggest source of budget overruns in traditional projects — essentially disappear. Value engineering happens at the drawing stage when it costs nothing. The owner's total management burden is also dramatically reduced.
Absolutely. Turn-key doesn't mean surrendering control — it means you have a single expert team to collaborate with. You make all aesthetic, functional, and strategic decisions. We guide you on code requirements, budget implications of different choices, and construction feasibility. The result is better-informed decisions, not fewer decisions.

We build backwards from your target opening or occupancy date and provide a specific milestone schedule at the start of every project.
